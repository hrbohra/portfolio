## What it is

<Plain>
The online store's 6,400 products had to come from somewhere. They live with 12 different
suppliers, each publishing their range a different way: password-protected trade websites,
glossy PDF brochures, spreadsheets, and one Italian manufacturer's site. As a stocking
retailer, my client holds trade accounts with all of them, and the suppliers consented to us
automating what would otherwise be months of manual catalogue entry.
</Plain>
<Eng>
ETL pipelines normalising 12 heterogeneous supplier sources (trade portals accessed with
retailer-issued credentials, brochure PDFs, spreadsheets) into one product schema that feeds
the storefront's offline catalogue builder. Supplier consent throughout.
</Eng>

## What was built

<Plain>
A family of pipelines, each shaped to its source. The trade-portal work is written up as its
own case: over a thousand products and nearly ten thousand photographs brought across with
zero errors from a server that could not take pressure. The brochure work turned nine PDF
catalogues into clean structured data, including reading text out of photographs of pages.
Around a thousand mattress images were cleaned up and given fresh descriptions.
</Plain>
<Eng>
Idempotent, checkpointed acquisition passes with manifests as the source of truth (the
fragile-server case documents the pattern). Nine supplier brochure PDFs parsed to structured
text and images via pdfplumber, PyPDF2 and pytesseract, including per-layout parsers where
generic bounding-box heuristics failed. About 1,000 mattress images processed for watermark
and swatch cleanup with rewritten SEO descriptions. An Italian manufacturer's catalogue built
from a single URL: 192 models, 3,075 variants, 814 HD images, 2,327 schematic drawings,
dimension tables in cm and inches, and an Italian-to-English glossary. Zero errors on both
flagship acquisitions.
</Eng>
