## What it is

<Plain>
My master's dissertation was not a paper exercise. It was delivered for the Built
Environment Trust, a real organisation, with real stakeholder meetings. The idea: give
London schoolchildren a map where they can pin places in their neighbourhood and record
what they noticed and how it made them feel. The result is a picture of a city as its
children experience it, useful to educators and planners.
</Plain>
<Eng>
Full-stack geo-emotion mapping platform. Flask and PostgreSQL backend, Folium/Leaflet maps,
Redis caching, Elasticsearch search. Children place markers with text and images; spaCy NLP
aggregates emotional signal across contributions.
</Eng>

## The hard part: children and privacy

<Plain>
Everything about this project is shaped by one fact: the users are children. Photos are
checked automatically before anyone sees them. Names and personal details are stripped from
anything that leaves the system. Partway through, the client meetings surfaced a simpler,
safer shape for the whole product, and I pivoted the design rather than defending the plan.
</Plain>
<Eng>
GDPR privacy-by-design: NSFW image moderation (TensorFlow.js and SightEngine), PII
anonymisation via Presidio on any exported insight, and data minimisation throughout.
Iterative client engagement, requirements meetings, prototype reviews, and a
feasibility-driven scope pivot in August 2024 that simplified the role model. The
production evolution of this platform became InvestigationMap during my internship at
Cardiff University's School of Architecture, where sentiment throughput reached 2,900+
entries per second, a 25 percent improvement.
</Eng>
