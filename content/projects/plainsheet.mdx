## What it is

<Plain>
Before I was an engineer, I spent two and a half years on a Cardiff University Public
Advisory Group for a cancer study, reading trial information sheets by hand to check that
ordinary people could actually understand them. PlainSheet is that job, automated. A trial
participant asks questions about their information sheet and gets answers at the reading
level they choose, with a citation to the exact paragraph. Ask it for medical advice and it
refuses, every time, and tells you who to ask instead. Study teams get a report on the sheet
itself: which sections are hard to read, which words are jargon.
</Plain>
<Eng>
A multi-step tool-using agent over ingested participant information sheets. Hand-written
orchestration loop (bounded, max 6 steps) over the tool-use stop reason, with a typed tool
registry, per-step trace events surfaced live in the UI, and hard system rules: every claim
cites a retrieved chunk, say so when the document does not contain the answer, never give
medical advice. Hybrid retrieval: pgvector cosine similarity fused with Postgres full-text
search via reciprocal rank fusion, because clinical documents punish embedding blur on exact
terms like drug names and visit numbers. Local MiniLM embeddings behind a port. An MCP
server exposes the same use cases to any MCP client.
</Eng>

## How it was made

<Plain>
It was built in three evenings, and the point was to build it the formal way at speed: a
design document before code, a launch checklist before the link went out, and a public test
suite that grades the AI's answers on every change. The test suite earned its keep on day
one, catching two real bugs the moment it first ran.
</Plain>
<Eng>
Hexagonal monorepo: a framework-free core defining ports (ChunkRepository, LlmProvider,
EmbeddingProvider, Logger, Clock), adapters for Postgres and two LLM vendors, one
composition root. The agent loop is tested against in-memory fakes; the Postgres adapter
against a real pgvector instance in CI. Provider-agnostic by demonstration, not claim: the
Gemini adapter was added after the Anthropic one without touching a line of core. Cost-aware
model split, cheap model for tool steps, stronger model for the user-facing answer, with
cost and latency logged per step. Evals as a first-class discipline: golden sets per sheet
including adversarial advice-seeking and not-in-document cases; scorers for citation
faithfulness, refusal correctness, reading-level compliance, latency and cost; results
written to a versioned RESULTS.md in CI so regressions show up in a PR diff. Current run:
25/25. Design decisions documented with rejected alternatives, including a semantic answer
cache rejected on safety grounds and fine-tuning rejected with reasons. DESIGN.md, a
production readiness review, a runbook and gitleaks/CodeQL in CI are all in the public repo.
</Eng>
