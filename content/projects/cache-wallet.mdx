## What it is

<Plain>
A mobile crypto wallet startup brought me in as founding engineer. The codebase had been
abandoned mid-flight by a previous team. My job was to make the product real: working, safe,
and ready to launch. I was the sole engineer across the backend, the mobile app, the smart
contracts and the infrastructure.
</Plain>
<Eng>
Sole engineer across four surfaces: NestJS/PostgreSQL backend, React Native (Expo) mobile
client, Solidity contracts, and infrastructure. Inherited an abandoned codebase; delivered a
launch-ready wallet spanning 7 EVM networks.
</Eng>

## What was built

<Plain>
The headline feature lets people swap one token for another without holding the special coin
usually needed to pay network fees. That sounds small and is quietly hard: it has to work the
same way across seven different blockchains, each with its own quirks. I also built the way
money enters the wallet from a bank card, a four-level rewards programme, and the account
recovery system for people who lose access.
</Plain>
<Eng>
Gasless swaps, same-chain and cross-chain: quote aggregation, EIP-712 signed orders, and a
provider-agnostic execution layer, so the mobile client never knows which liquidity source
fills it. Per-chain RPC routing absorbs gas estimation, nonce and signature-recovery quirks
behind one internal API. ERC-20 allowance and approval calldata builders. On-device key
custody: private keys never leave the device. Fiat on-ramp aggregation via Meld (Banxa,
Transak, MoonPay) with widget sessions and webhook receivers, plus an off-ramp foundation
with a provider-agnostic interface and mock client that let frontend and backend proceed in
parallel before provider selection. A four-tier NFT loyalty and on-chain referral system
(capped supply, Thirdweb signature minting, EIP-712 vouchers). Solidity V2 modules:
RecoverySwitch and ScheduledTransfer with a two-step on-chain enable and Safe multi-sig
management, deployed to Base Sepolia and ETH Sepolia, with a pre-mainnet compatibility audit
that flagged oracle-staleness and fee-collection risks.
</Eng>
