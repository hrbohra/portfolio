<Plain>
I came to software the long way round. A psychology degree in Bangalore, a year teaching
maths and science to schoolchildren, business development at a jewellery and finance house
where I helped build my first commercial system, a CRM the staff actually used. Then a
master's in computing at Cardiff, a dissertation delivered to a real client, and since
then: two founding-engineer roles at startups and a freelance practice where I have taken a
furniture retailer's entire digital life from paper to production.

The unusual path is the point. I can sit with a shop owner, a founder or a researcher and
hear what they actually need, then go away and build all of it. The rest of this site is
the evidence.

Away from work: I edited a published novel, The Eyes of Justicia, and I spent two and a
half years as a public representative on a cancer research study, helping scientists
explain their work to the people it affects.
</Plain>
